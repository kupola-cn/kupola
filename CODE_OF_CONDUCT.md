# Code of Conduct

## Our standards

Project participants are expected to communicate respectfully, accept technical
feedback in good faith, and focus criticism on code and decisions rather than
people. Harassment, discrimination, threats, deliberate disruption, and the
publication of another person's private information are not acceptable.

Examples of positive participation include:

- Providing reproducible technical evidence and constructive alternatives.
- Respecting differing experience levels, backgrounds, and viewpoints.
- Acknowledging mistakes and helping resolve their impact.
- Keeping issues, reviews, and discussions relevant to the project.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove comments, commits, issues, and other contributions
that violate these standards. Repeated or serious violations may result in a
temporary or permanent ban from project spaces.

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository owners through their GitHub
profiles. Do not use a public issue when disclosure could expose or further harm
another participant. Reports will be reviewed confidentially and without
retaliation.

This policy applies in repository spaces and when representing the project in
public spaces.
